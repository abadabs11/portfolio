Overview

Sametz Blackstone Associates is a small design firm which focuses on strategy based work, mainly for nonprofit and education based companies. I worked at Sametz for 6 months as a full-time co-op, during which I had the opportunity to lead projects, work directly with clients, and contribute to projects ranging from a real estate company’s rebrand to a medical company’s website redesign.

My Role

At Sametz I collaborated with a team of designers, developers, and strategists on a wide variety of print and web-based design campaigns. Under the direction of the Design Director I led projects from strategy to production.

Things I Learned

  • Respect for “perfect” design
    Every rag, every byline, every margin has a place and a reason. Craft is king.
  • How to work with (and within) a brand
    Creativity within limits. How to expand while staying on brand
  • Be proud of the work your client does

Case Study

Olin College of Engineering • Capabilities Brochure
During my time at Sametz I was incredibly drawn to print design for it’s permanence, finality, and willingness to let you have control over every tiny detail, down to every last bit of kerning.

I especially loved print design that had the courage to break out of a typical page format and wow the viewer. Inspired by the likes of Post Typography, _____, and Stefan Sagmeister, I really wanted to experiment with print design as a more physical/experiential being.

This is why I was particularly excited by my first major project at Sametz, creating Olin College of Engineering’s 2014 Capabilities Brochure.

Olin is a college known for its innovative atmosphere and bold personality. Under the direction Alex Budnitz, the Director of Design, we created a brochure for their 2015 school year that encapsulated Olin’s 6 core characteristics: __, __, __, __, __, and __. Alex conducted initial meetings with the client and put together the concept and form for the brochure – it would be an oversized booklet with 6 separate fold-out sections, not including front and back fold-out covers. Talk about wow factor!

Working within Olin's branding, I put together proposals for several different concepts on how to best visually represent these characteristics. With further stakeholder discussions and client approval it was decided to move forward with the theme of various geometric themes morphing across the sections highlighting the college’s move away from stereotypical characteristics (ie. shallow) to their own (i.e. immersive).

Creating this piece in Olin’s signature heavily layered and ultimately ___ style pushed the limits of my Swiss school upbringing, but eventually led to an amazing pay-off.

When it came time to ship this baby I had my first opportunity to see it through all the way to ink by spending the first day of production checking proofs on the floor in the print room. One of the coolest things to see come directly off the press was the shimmery, translucent silver accent color strung throughout.
